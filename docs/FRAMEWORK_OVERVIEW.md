# EstateLab Seven-Stage Framework

## Status

This is the canonical and complete foundational structure of EstateLab.

Future founder insights, external evidence, and investment outcomes should refine the relevant stage. EstateLab should not add another stage unless a genuine structural gap is identified and the framework is explicitly redesigned.

## Stage 1: Property Selection

Primary file: `docs/MY_INVESTMENT_FRAMEWORK.md`

Determines whether the property itself deserves consideration through area demand, price segment, buyer pool, own-stay appeal, project and unit quality, evidence, rental resilience, supply, title, hard stops, and final scoring.

## Stage 2: Investor Suitability

Primary file: `docs/INVESTOR_MANDATE_PROFILE.md`

Determines whether the user is financially, behaviourally, and strategically suitable for the property and mandate.

## Stage 3: Financing And Deal Structure

Primary file: `docs/DEAL_STRUCTURING_FINANCING.md`

Determines whether leverage, cash outlay, valuation, debt service, renovation cost, and any financing-led purpose are transparent and survivable.

## Stage 4: Holding Power And Asset Management

Primary file: `docs/HOLDING_POWER_ASSET_MANAGEMENT.md`

Determines whether the investor can operate, review, hold, refinance, or sell the asset without being forced into a bad decision.

## Stage 5: Portfolio Strategy And Scaling

Primary file: `docs/PORTFOLIO_STRATEGY_SCALING.md`

Determines whether the investor has earned the right to add another property while controlling reserve, concentration, total debt, and capital-recycling risk.

## Stage 6: Market Intelligence And Timing

Primary file: `docs/MARKET_INTELLIGENCE_TIMING.md`

Interprets macro conditions, local cycles, supply timing, rental direction, buyer liquidity, policy, credit, fear, and hype without allowing timing to rescue a weak asset.

## Stage 7: Decision Journal And Second-Brain Learning

Primary file: `docs/DECISION_JOURNAL_LEARNING.md`

Records predictions before outcomes, separates process from profit and luck, reviews results, and updates beliefs when evidence disagrees.

## Cross-Stage Layer: Execution Calibration

Primary file: `docs/EXECUTION_CALIBRATION.md`

Calibrates how Apex interprets deal sources, agent behaviour, seller motivation, offer strategy, and negotiation pressure. This is not an eighth stage. It sharpens Stages 1, 3, and 7 by separating genuine opportunity from sales claims, public advertisement noise, and user FOMO.

## Required Decision Order

EstateLab should normally move through the stages in order:

1. Does the property deserve to be bought?
2. Is this investor suitable?
3. Is the financing safe and transparent?
4. Can the investor hold and manage it?
5. Does it improve or weaken the portfolio?
6. Does current timing improve or damage the case?
7. What prediction, counter-thesis, kill criteria, and review record must be preserved?

A later-stage advantage cannot normally rescue an earlier-stage hard stop. Attractive financing does not repair a weak property. Strong market timing does not repair an unsuitable investor. A high score does not override unresolved legal, structural, transaction, or survival risk.
