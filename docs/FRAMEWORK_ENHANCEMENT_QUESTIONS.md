# Framework Enhancement Questions

## Purpose Of This File

This file is a structured set of questions intended to enhance the existing v1 framework. It is not a new framework, a new stage, or a set of new rules. It exists because `docs/FRAMEWORK_OVERVIEW.md` states that EstateLab should extend a stage only when a *genuine structural gap* is identified, and that the framework improves through founder insight, external evidence, and recorded outcomes.

Each section below names a gap, explains why it matters, points at the stage or engine version it would extend, and asks the founder the questions whose answers would close it. These questions are deliberately the kind that could change a score, a hard stop, an evidence gate, or a decision boundary — not cosmetic detail.

The questions are ordered roughly by how structurally important the gap is to the current framework.

---

## 1. New-Launch And Under-Construction (Off-Plan) Decisions

**Gap.** The entire evidence engine (`v4.0`–`v4.6`) and Stage 1's site-visit veto assume a *completed* property. A new launch bought off-plan has no achieved rent, no occupancy, no observable management, no resident behaviour, no lived site visit, and no completed-transaction comparables for that exact product. Under the current rules almost every evidence gate would be blocked, yet a large share of Malaysian retail purchases are new launches. The framework currently has no first-class path for this, only warnings about developer discounts and bulk purchase.

**Extends.** Stage 1 (`MY_INVESTMENT_FRAMEWORK.md`), Stage 6 (`MARKET_INTELLIGENCE_TIMING.md`), the evidence engine (`APEX_DECISION_STANDARD.md`).

1. When no completed comparable exists for the exact new product, what substitute evidence (nearby completed projects, developer's prior delivered projects, surrounding rental) should replace the `v4.1` completed-comparable gate, and at what confidence ceiling?
2. What is the maximum confidence Apex should ever assign to an off-plan deal where rent, management, and lived quality cannot yet be observed?
3. How should construction risk be scored — late delivery (LAD entitlement), redesign, contractor change, developer gearing, abandonment, or conversion to a deferred project?
4. What developer-solvency checks are realistic for a retail user (track record of delivered projects, financial filings, sales-and-purchase track record, ongoing project count) and which are mandatory before any off-plan recommendation?
5. How should developer rebates, free legal fees, rebates packaged into price, and "free" furnishing be stripped out to find the true net entry price?
6. Should off-plan purchases be capped to specific investor profiles (e.g. growth-focused or opportunistic with strong reserve) given the holding period extends through a construction window with no rental income?
7. What is the founder's rule on progressive-payment cash-flow during construction versus a completed property that produces rent from day one?

## 2. Auction And Distressed Acquisition Playbook

**Gap.** Auction prices are treated as the *strongest* value evidence and auctions appear as an opportunistic strategy, but there is no dedicated playbook for actually *buying* at auction. Auction purchases carry unique, non-obvious risks the framework does not yet encode.

**Extends.** Stage 1K legal/transactionability, Stage 2 opportunistic profile, `EXECUTION_CALIBRATION.md`.

1. How should Apex handle the buyer's inability to inspect the interior of most auction units before bidding — does this raise or override the site-visit veto?
2. How should outstanding charges assumed by the successful bidder (maintenance arrears, sinking fund, quit rent, assessment, utilities, and sometimes redemption shortfall) be estimated and added to true entry cost before setting a maximum bid?
3. What is the rule when the property is still occupied by the former owner or a tenant — how should eviction time, cost, and risk be priced?
4. How should the 10% deposit and the 90/120-day balance settlement deadline interact with the financing-readiness gate, given auction financing must be pre-arranged?
5. When does a low reserve price signal genuine value versus a title, access, or legal defect that scared away other bidders?
6. How many auction cases in one project should move from "isolated owner distress" to a project-level hard stop, normalised by total units and purchase cohort?

## 3. Taxation, RPGT, And Holding-Period Timing

**Gap.** Real Property Gains Tax (RPGT) is effectively absent, yet it directly shapes the founder's own 3–7 year holding rule and every exit decision. Rental-income tax and the personal-versus-company ownership choice are also missing from the cost schedule, so "net ROI" cannot truly be computed.

**Extends.** Stage 3 (`DEAL_STRUCTURING_FINANCING.md` cash-cost schedule), Stage 4 hold/sell, Stage 7 return-definition discipline.

1. How should the RPGT tier schedule (higher tax in the early years, lower after the holding threshold) be built into the disposal-timing and walk-away math, so Apex never recommends a sale year that quietly destroys net gain?
2. Should the decision record's "target disposal year" be tested explicitly against the RPGT step-down, and should an early forced sale trigger a recalculated net return?
3. How should taxable rental income and allowable deductions (interest, assessment, quit rent, maintenance, repairs, agent fees) enter the net-return formula so `v4.2` rent coverage reflects after-tax reality?
4. When should Apex compare individual ownership against a Sdn Bhd structure (loan margin, RPGT treatment, rental-income tax, compliance cost), and is company ownership inside or outside the retail-investor default?
5. How do stamp duty on transfer and on the loan agreement, plus the legal disbursement schedule, scale by price band, and should these be auto-estimated rather than left to the user?

## 4. Financing Mechanics Beyond Interest Rate

**Gap.** Stage 3 rightly warns retail investors not to fixate on interest rate, but the framework itself does not yet distinguish the financing *structures* a Malaysian buyer actually chooses between, several of which change holding strategy materially.

**Extends.** Stage 3 (`DEAL_STRUCTURING_FINANCING.md`), Stage 4 holding power, Stage 5 capital recycling.

1. How should conventional versus Islamic financing (e.g. profit-rate ceilings, BBA/Murabahah/Tawarruq mechanics) be compared when both are offered, and does either change the stress-test logic?
2. How should a full-flexi or semi-flexi loan with an offset account change the holding-power and reserve calculation, since parked cash reduces effective interest while staying liquid?
3. What is the rule for lock-in periods and exit penalties when the planned holding period is short, or when refinancing is part of the Stage 5 plan?
4. How should joint borrowing (spouse, family, partner) affect DSR, the reserve gate, and the exit case when co-owners may later disagree?
5. How should MRTA versus MLTA be chosen, and how does a lump-sum MRTA financed into the loan change the true all-in cost and the refinance math later?
6. How should existing guarantor obligations or other contingent liabilities be folded into the danger-zone DSR test?

## 5. Climate, Flood, And Physical Environmental Risk

**Gap.** Flood risk appears only once, as a single phrase in portfolio concentration. After repeated major Malaysian flood events this is a live, location-specific, and largely permanent risk that affects insurance, financing, rent, resident behaviour, and resale — exactly the kind of unchangeable defect Stage 1 says to find before relying on renovation or yield.

**Extends.** Stage 1B area selection, Stage 1E own-stay/site visit, Stage 4 holding cost, Stage 6 area risk.

1. Should flood history (flash-flood frequency, drainage, basement car-park exposure, past depth) be a scored micro-location criterion or, above a threshold, a hard stop?
2. How should hillside, slope, and landslide-prone siting be evaluated, including past slope failures and ongoing earthworks nearby?
3. How should flood or slope risk feed insurance cost and availability, and feed the future-buyer-financing and resale-objection tests?
4. What ground evidence at site visit signals chronic water problems beyond a single unit (drain marks, basement pump history, management's flood response record)?
5. Should climate exposure carry a holding-cost reserve (higher insurance, repair frequency) inside the `v1.6` stress envelope?

## 6. Strata Governance And Short-Term-Rental Regulation

**Gap.** The framework reads JMB quality through observable behaviour, which is strong, but it does not encode the Strata Management Act mechanics that determine whether a building can actually fix itself — and it flags short-stay products as risky without addressing the shifting legality of short-term letting.

**Extends.** Stage 1F project quality, Stage 4 asset management, Stage 1D buyer-pool concentration.

1. How should the JMB-to-Management-Corporation transition stage, share-unit allocation, AGM participation, and Strata Management Tribunal disputes be checked as governance-health evidence?
2. What level of sinking-fund balance relative to building age and component-replacement need should count as healthy versus a future special-levy risk?
3. How should building by-laws and house rules that restrict renovation, letting, or pets affect rentability and the renovation plan?
4. Given that short-term-rental (Airbnb-style) legality varies by state, by building by-law, and by title use, how should Apex treat a thesis that depends on short-stay income, and should such dependence cap confidence?
5. How should a recent or pending management handover, developer-controlled JMB, or unpaid developer maintenance contribution be scored?

## 7. Geographic Scope Beyond The Founder Default

**Gap.** The framework is calibrated to Penang, KL, and Selangor high-rise residential. It states preferences are mandate-specific, but it offers little machinery for users in materially different markets — especially Johor/Iskandar (foreign-heavy, oversupplied condo history, RTS/data-centre catalysts) and Sabah/Sarawak, where land law itself differs from the Peninsular National Land Code.

**Extends.** Stage 1A/1B selection philosophy and area selection, Stage 1K legal/title.

1. Sabah (Land Ordinance) and Sarawak (Land Code) operate under different land law, native title categories, and consent regimes than the Peninsular NLC — what title and transactionability checks change for a user investing there?
2. How should Johor/Iskandar's foreign-buyer concentration, historic high-rise oversupply, and specific catalysts (RTS Link, data centres, special economic zone) be weighed without importing KL/Penang assumptions?
3. How should the area-selection rules adapt to landed-dominant or growth-corridor markets where the founder's mature-rental-area defaults do not apply?
4. What is the minimum local-knowledge or local-evidence standard before Apex should speak with confidence in a market outside the founder's three-state core?

## 8. Foreign-Buyer Rules And Exit-Pool Eligibility

**Gap.** "Foreigners, where local rules, pricing, and product type allow" appears as a buyer segment but is never developed, even though state-set minimum purchase prices for foreigners and consent rules directly constrain the *exit* buyer pool — which the framework treats as central.

**Extends.** Stage 1D future buyer pool, Stage 2.4 exit strategy.

1. How should state-specific foreign-buyer minimum price floors affect the future-buyer-pool breadth test, especially for units priced near or below those floors?
2. When a thesis relies on foreign exit demand, what evidence of actual foreign transaction activity (not marketing) should be required, and how should currency and policy risk be flagged?
3. How should MM2H-driven demand be treated given its eligibility terms have changed repeatedly — is dependence on it a confidence cap?
4. How should developer foreign-quota units and bumiputera-release timing affect resale liquidity and pricing?

---

## 9. Rental Operations And Tenancy Legal Mechanics

**Gap.** Tenant *fit* and behaviour are covered well, but the legal and operational mechanics of letting in Malaysia — where there is no central tenancy registration and limited statutory tenant protection — are largely absent. These mechanics affect vacancy cost, recovery of arrears, and realised net return.

**Extends.** Stage 2.3 tenant/rental plan, Stage 4 holding power.

1. What deposit structure and tenancy-agreement terms should Apex treat as standard, and how should the absence of a formal eviction statute (reliance on the Distress Act / civil action) change the vacancy and bad-tenant cost assumption?
2. How should the cost and time of recovering possession from a defaulting tenant be priced into the holding-power stress case?
3. How should rooms-versus-whole-unit letting change the rent, vacancy, management-effort, and wear assumptions?
4. How should a property manager's fee and quality be scored when the owner is not local to the asset?

## 10. Quantitative Scoring And Confidence Calibration

**Gap.** The scorecard weights, the 0–5 scale, the score thresholds (Shortlist ≥74, confidence ≥65, evidence ≥80/100), and the blocker confidence cap (64%) all exist, but the *mapping* from raw field observations to a 0–5 sub-score, and the method for combining conflicting evidence sources into one confidence number, are underspecified. Two analysts could score the same deal very differently.

**Extends.** Stage 1L final scoring, evidence engine, `v3.6` source transparency.

1. For each scorecard criterion, what concrete observation corresponds to each point on the 0–5 scale, so scoring is repeatable rather than intuitive?
2. How should conflicting evidence sources be weighted into a single confidence figure (source reliability × recency × relevance), and is there a worked weighting the engine should use?
3. How should "unknown" (score 0) propagate — does a single critical unknown cap the whole confidence, and which unknowns are critical versus tolerable?
4. How stale is too stale, per evidence type, before a previously strong gate degrades (the `v4.1`/`v4.2` 12-month and 6-month windows imply a decay curve that could be generalised)?
5. Should Apex expose a sensitivity view — which single assumption, if wrong, most changes the verdict — rather than only deterministic point stress tests?

---

## 11. Exit And Disposal Execution

**Gap.** Stages 4, 6, and 7 decide *when* and *whether* to sell and stress saleability hard, but there is no execution layer for the *act* of selling, in contrast to the detailed `v1.5` buy-side execution calibration.

**Extends.** Stage 2.4 exit strategy, Stage 4 sell/hold, `EXECUTION_CALIBRATION.md`.

1. What is the disciplined disposal posture analogous to the buy-side: pre-sale preparation, pricing anchor against completed comps, exclusive versus open agent listing, and a minimum-acceptable-price walk-away?
2. How should vacant-versus-tenanted disposal be decided, given an in-place tenant narrows the buyer pool to investors but vacant carrying cost burns reserve?
3. What staging, light-renovation, and presentation spend is justified at exit, and how is its uplift evidenced rather than assumed?
4. How should RPGT filing timing and the net-of-tax proceeds shape the asking-price and accept/reject decision at sale?
5. When should a price reduction be made decisively versus held, mirroring the existing rent-reduction logic in Stage 4?

---

## 12. Data Sources And The Open Research Items

**Gap.** `MARKET_INTELLIGENCE_TIMING.md` explicitly leaves two founder questions as **open research items** (which economic changes most affect Malaysian property demand; which employment sectors create the most dependable housing demand in Penang, KL, and Selangor). More broadly, the framework names trusted sources (Brickz, NAPIC/JPPH, auction pages) but has no standard for source freshness, coverage gaps, or what to do when a source is unreachable.

**Extends.** Stage 1G evidence, Stage 6 evidence status, `v2.0` market console.

1. What current, dated evidence should resolve the two open research items, and what source hierarchy (DOSM, BNM, NAPIC/JPPH, MIDA, state agencies) should Apex cite when it does?
2. For each evidence type, what is the authoritative source, its typical lag, and its known blind spots, so Apex can state coverage honestly?
3. How should Apex behave when the best source is unreachable or out of date — what fallback evidence is acceptable and at what confidence penalty?
4. Which employment and demand engines (data centres, semiconductor and E&E clusters, universities, healthcare, logistics, government) have proven the most durable rental demand, and how should each be weighted by area?
5. How frequently should owner market observations (`v2.0`) be re-verified per evidence type before they are surfaced as stale?

## How To Use This File

1. Treat each answered question as a candidate refinement to the named stage or engine version, not as automatically authoritative.
2. Convert a founder answer into framework text only when it reveals a repeatable market mechanism, consistent with the working method in `MY_INVESTMENT_FRAMEWORK.md`.
3. Where an answer would create a new boundary, decide explicitly whether it is a hard stop, a pause, a blocker, a scored criterion, or a confidence cap, so it slots into the existing decision order.
4. Keep `docs/FRAMEWORK_OVERVIEW.md`'s discipline: deepen the existing stages and engine rather than adding a new stage unless a genuine structural gap remains after these questions are answered.
